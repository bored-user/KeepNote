å leke vs å spille

While “å leke" is used on more general playing (like kids on a playground), “å spille” is used on rule-based games. For instance: one can “spille” the chess and “leke” hide and seek.

As a general rule, “spille” is used for computer games, sports and instruments; while “leke” is more for child-related games. Note, however, that the Olympic Games are “leke(d)” instead of “spille(d)”.
