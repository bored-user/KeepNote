Definite plural

The definite plural form convention is the way of defining plural nouns. It basically consist in pluralizing the noun and replacing the “er” ending with the letters “ene” or “ne” - if the noun already ends in “e” or “r” (or even “a” if the noun is neutral, even though the first is more common).

For instance: “katt” means “cat”, while “kattene” means “the cats”. There are, however, irregular plural nouns, like “mann” ("man"), which becomes “mennene” ("men").
